Before You Order

Start with a quote request — tell us the garment, quantity, print locations, and your artwork or idea. We respond to quote requests within 24 hours; on busier weeks it can take up to 2 business days. If you haven't heard back by then, call us directly at (216) 223-8050. The more complete your request, the faster and more accurate your quote.
Standard production turnaround is 10–14 business days once your order is fully approved and confirmed. Orders are not confirmed until payment has been made or arranged — that's the clock-start. Estimated completion does not include shipping. "Completed on time" is the philosophy, and organized workflows keep every order moving to its date.
Screen printing has a minimum of 24 garments for a 1-color print; minimums rise with artwork complexity and the number of print locations. Embroidery starts at 12 pieces. Direct-to-film handles smaller, detail-heavy runs. Tell us what you're working with and we'll match the method to your quantity and budget.
Submit hi-resolution PNG, PSD, PDF, or AI files, and label each file with your brand, business, or first and last name. Don't have print-ready vector art? We can vectorize and adjust your artwork — standard art fees apply. Whether it was made in Canva, generated with AI, or designed years ago, our File Refinement process rebuilds every element for a clean print.
Standard prints run up to 12.75"W × 14.5"H. Oversized prints run up to 15.5"W × 18"H. Joggers and sleeves can be printed up to 17" in length. Not sure your design fits? Send it over and we'll confirm placement before production.
We don't hold blank inventory — every garment is sourced specifically for your order through our partnered wholesale vendors (see the Resources page). Need help picking the right blank? We'll guide you. You're also welcome to supply your own garments; a slight check-in/out charge applies based on quantity and packaging, and specialty or vintage pieces may require a signed waiver.
Screen printing delivers soft-hand, retail-quality prints at volume. Embroidery suits caps, uniforms, and premium apparel. Direct-to-film handles full-color, detail-heavy artwork and smaller runs. We'll match the method to your design and quantity.
